# LocalSkill Demo Video Script (Simple Version)

Target length: about 6-7 minutes. Record your phone or emulator screen. You can
talk while recording, or record the screen first and add your voice later. Each
part below tells you what to **show** and what to **say**.

**Record each numbered part as its own short video.** Then join them together at
the end. This way, if you make a mistake, you only redo that one small part, not
the whole video.

### Parts list

| # | Part | Time |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Intro | 25-35 sec |
| 2 | Login | 25-30 sec |
| 3 | Job Seeker | 100-140 sec |
| 4 | Company | 100-140 sec |
| 5 | Admin | 80-110 sec |
| 6 | Ending | 25-35 sec |

### Before you record

- Have 3 accounts ready: one Job Seeker, one Company (already verified/approved),
  one Admin.
- Have 1 more Company account that is **not verified yet**. You will use this to
  show how verification works.
- Post 2-3 jobs before you record, so the lists are not empty.
- Turn off notifications on your phone/emulator so nothing pops up during
  recording.

---

## 1. Intro (25-35 sec)

**Show:** App icon on the home screen → open the app → splash screen with the logo
→ role selection screen.

**Say:**
> "This is LocalSkill. It is an Android app that connects job seekers with
> verified companies. It also has an admin side to keep everything safe and
> correct. The app is built with Kotlin and Jetpack Compose, and it uses Firebase
> for login and data storage. There are three types of users: Job Seeker, Company,
> and Admin."

---

## 2. Login (25-30 sec)

**Show:** Swipe through the onboarding screens → role selection → login screen →
log in as Job Seeker.

**Say:**
> "When you open the app the first time, you see a few intro screens. Then you
> choose if you are a Job Seeker or a Company. Admin accounts are not made this
> way — an admin has to set those up directly, for safety. Login uses Firebase, and
> it shows clear error messages if something is wrong."

---

## 3. Job Seeker (100-140 sec)

**Show:** You are logged in as Job Seeker, on the Home screen.

### 3a. Home Screen
**Show:** Home screen with featured jobs, recent jobs, and category icons.

**Say:**
> "The home screen shows featured jobs and recent jobs. You can also browse by
> category — each category now has its own icon, so it's easy to tell them apart.
> It also shows how complete your profile is, so you know what to finish."

### 3b. Search
**Show:** Open Search → type something → use a filter (like job type or
location).

**Say:**
> "You can search for jobs by typing, and also filter by job type, work type, or
> location."

### 3c. Apply for a Job
**Show:** Tap a job → job details page → tap Apply → write a short cover letter →
submit.

**Say:**
> "Job details show everything about the job. Applying takes one tap. You cannot
> apply to the same job twice, and you must add a resume first before you can
> apply."

### 3d. Profile
**Show:** Go to Profile → show Personal Info / Education / Experience / Skills /
Resume → add a skill → open Resume and show how to upload one.

**Say:**
> "Your profile has separate sections: personal info, education, experience, and
> skills. For your resume, you can upload a PDF, a Word file, or even a photo of a
> printed resume."

### 3e. Applications and Saved Jobs
**Show:** Applications tab (show an application and its status) → Saved Jobs tab.

**Say:**
> "You can track your applications — applied, under review, shortlisted,
> interview, hired, or rejected. You can also save jobs to look at later."

---

## 4. Company (100-140 sec)

**Show:** Log out, then log in with the **new, not-verified** Company account.

### 4a. Verification
**Show:** Company Dashboard shows "Verification required" → open Verification
screen → fill in the missing info → upload the registration certificate → tap
"Submit for verification."

**Say:**
> "A company cannot post any jobs until it is verified. First, the company profile
> must be fully filled out. Then a registration certificate must be uploaded. Only
> after that can they submit for verification. This keeps fake companies off the
> app."

### 4b. Admin Approves
**Show:** Switch to the Admin account → Companies tab → Pending filter → tap the
green check mark on the company.

**Say:**
> "On the admin side, you can approve or reject a company right from the list. You
> don't need to open each one separately. It updates right away."

### 4c. Post a Job
**Show:** Switch back to the Company account → Dashboard now shows verified → Jobs
tab → Post a Job → fill the form (title, category, description, location, job
type, salary, deadline) → Publish.

**Say:**
> "Now that the company is verified, it can post a job. You fill in the title,
> category, description, location, job type, salary range, and deadline. The app
> checks everything before it goes live."

### 4d. Manage Applicants
**Show:** Applicants tab → open one applicant → change status to "Shortlisted" →
schedule an interview.

**Say:**
> "Companies can review each applicant, move them through the hiring steps, and
> schedule interviews. The job seeker gets notified every time their status
> changes."

---

## 5. Admin (80-110 sec)

**Show:** Admin Dashboard — stats, pending companies, recent activity.

**Say:**
> "The admin dashboard shows a quick view of the whole app — total users,
> companies, jobs, open reports, and suspended accounts. Important numbers are
> shown in color so they stand out. There is also a log of everything the admin
> has done."

**Show:** Jobs tab → remove a job and give a reason → Users tab → suspend a user →
Reports tab → open and resolve a report → Categories tab → add a new category.

**Say:**
> "Admins can also remove bad job posts, suspend user accounts, resolve reports
> from users, and manage the list of job categories."

---

## 6. Ending (25-35 sec)

**Show:** Quickly switch between the three dashboards (Job Seeker, Company,
Admin).

**Say:**
> "That's LocalSkill. Job seekers can find and apply for jobs. Companies get
> verified and post jobs. Admins keep everything safe and running well. The app is
> tested with JUnit and Mockito for the code, and Espresso for the screens. It uses
> Firebase for login and data, and Cloudinary for storing files. Thanks for
> watching."

---

## Recording Tips

- If a loading spinner takes more than a second, cut that part out later. Don't
  talk over it.
- Move a little slower than feels normal. Fast taps look rushed on video.
- Practice each part once without recording, so you know exactly what to tap.
- If you make a mistake, stop and record that part again. Don't try to keep going.
